Traditional Medieval English Meat Pie

A meat pie is a dish of diced meat, vegetables, and spices baked inside a pastry crust. It is a hearty and filling meal that can be eaten hot or cold

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. of diced beef or pork, obtained from the local butcher
  • 2 onions, chopped, purchased from a nearby farmer or market
  • 2 carrots, chopped, obtained from a local farm
  • 2 cups of beef or chicken broth, made by boiling bones and vegetables in water
  • 1/2 cup of red wine, obtained from a local vineyard or merchant
  • 1 tsp. of dried thyme, purchased from an apothecary
  • 1 tsp. of dried rosemary, obtained from a nearby garden or market
  • Salt and pepper, to taste, obtained from a local salt merchant or spice trader
  • 2 pie crusts made from flour, butter, and water, which can be made at home or purchased from a local baker

Directions

Step 1

First, chop the onions and carrots with a sharp knife.

Step 2

Heat a large pot over a fire, and add the chopped onions and carrots to the pot. Stir occasionally until they start to soften.

Step 3

Add the diced meat to the pot and cook until it is browned on all sides.

Step 4

Add the beef or chicken broth, red wine, thyme, and rosemary to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 5

Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the meat is tender

Step 6

While the meat is cooking, make the pie crusts by combining flour, butter, and water in a bowl. Knead the dough until it is smooth, then roll it out with a rolling pin.

Step 7

Once the meat mixture is cooked, pour it into one of the pie crusts.

Step 8

Cover the meat mixture with the second pie crust, and crimp the edges together to seal them.

Step 9

Brush the beaten egg over the top of the pie crust.

Step 9

Bake the meat pie in a stone oven for about 45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.

Step 9

Let the pie cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy!
