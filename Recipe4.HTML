Meatloaf

Meatloaf is a hearty and filling dish made with ground beef, bread crumbs, and spices. It is a popular dish among peasants and lords alike.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. of ground beef, obtained from the local butcher
  • 1 cup of bread crumbs, made by drying out and crumbling bread
  • 1 onion, finely chopped, purchased from a nearby farmer or market
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced, obtained from a nearby garden or market
  • 2 eggs, obtained from your own chickens or a nearby farm
  • 1 tsp. of dried thyme, purchased from an apothecary
  • 1 tsp. of dried rosemary, obtained from a nearby garden or market
  • Salt and pepper, to taste, obtained from a local salt merchant or spice trader

Directions

Step 1

First, preheat the oven hot enough to burn a newts toes off.

Step 2

In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, bread crumbs, chopped onion, minced garlic, eggs, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper.

Step 3

Knead the mixture with your hands until it is well combined.

Step 4

Grease a baking dish with butter or lard.

Step 5

Place the meat mixture in the baking dish and shape it into a loaf.

Step 6

Bake the meatloaf in the preheated oven for about 1 hour, or until it is cooked through and golden brown on top.

Step 7

Let the meatloaf rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Step 8

Serve the meatloaf hot, accompanied by roasted vegetables or mashed potatoes.

Note:For a richer meatloaf, you can add bacon or sausage to the mixture, or use a mixture of beef and pork.
