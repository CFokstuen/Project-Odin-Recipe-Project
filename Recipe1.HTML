Beef Stew

A beef stew is a warm and nourishing dish made with beef, vegetables, and spices. It is a popular dish among peasants and lords alike.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. of beef, cut into small pieces, obtained from the local butcher
  • 2 onions, chopped, purchased from a nearby farmer or market
  • 2 carrots, chopped, obtained from a local farm
  • 2 potatoes, chopped, obtained from a local farm
  • 2 cups of beef broth, made by boiling bones and vegetables in water
  • 1 tsp. of dried thyme, purchased from an apothecary
  • 1 tsp. of dried rosemary, obtained from a nearby garden or market
  • Salt and pepper, to taste, obtained from a local salt merchant or spice trader

Directions

Step 1

First, chop the onions, carrots, and potatoes with a sharp knife.

Step 2

Heat a large pot over a fire, and add the chopped onions to the pot. Stir occasionally until they start to soften.

Step 3

Add the beef to the pot and cook until it is browned on all sides.

Step 4

Add the beef broth, thyme, and rosemary to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 5

Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 30 minutes.

Step 6

Add the chopped carrots and potatoes to the pot. Stir well.

Step 7

Continue to simmer the stew for another 30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the beef is cooked through.

Step 8

Serve the beef stew hot in wooden bowls, accompanied by crusty bread.

Note: If you prefer a thicker stew, you can add a tablespoon of flour or breadcrumbs to the pot while the stew is simmering.
