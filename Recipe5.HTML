Pea Soup

Pea soup is a hearty and nutritious dish made with split peas, vegetables, and herbs. It is a popular dish among peasants and lords alike.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of dried split peas, obtained from a local grain merchant
  • 6 cups of water, obtained from a nearby well or spring
  • 1 onion, chopped, purchased from a nearby farmer or market
  • 2 carrots, chopped, obtained from a nearby garden or market
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped, obtained from a nearby garden or market
  • 1 tsp. of dried thyme, purchased from an apothecary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste, obtained from a local salt merchant or spice trader

Directions

Step 1

First, rinse the split peas and soak them in water for at least 2 hours.

Step 2

Drain the split peas and place them in a large pot with 6 cups of water.

Step 3

Add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, thyme, salt, and pepper to the pot, and stir well.

Step 4

Place the pot over a fire and bring the mixture to a boil.

Step 5

Reduce the heat and let the soup simmer for about 1 hour, or until the peas are soft and the vegetables are tender.

Step 6

Use a wooden spoon or a whisk to mash the peas and vegetables into a smooth and creamy soup.

Step 7

If the soup is too thick, add more water until it reaches the desired consistency.

Step 8

Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning, if necessary.

Step 9

Remove the soup from the fire and let it cool for a few minutes.

Step 10

Ladle the pea soup into bowls and serve it hot, accompanied by crusty bread or cheese.

Note:For a heartier soup, you can add diced ham or bacon to the mixture, or use chicken or beef broth instead of water.
