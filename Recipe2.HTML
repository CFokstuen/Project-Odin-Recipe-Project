Frumenty Pudding

Frumenty pudding is a sweet and creamy dish made with wheat, milk, eggs, and spices. It is a popular dessert among nobles and peasants alike.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of whole wheat berries, obtained from a local grain merchant
  • 4 cups of milk, obtained from a local farmer
  • 4 egg yolks, obtained from your own chickens or a nearby farm
  • 1/2 cup of honey, obtained from a local apiary or market
  • 1 tsp. of ground cinnamon, obtained from a local spice trader or apothecary
  • 1 tsp. of ground ginger, obtained from a nearby garden or market

Directions

Step 1

First, rinse the wheat berries and soak them overnight in water.

Step 2

Drain the wheat berries, and place them in a large pot with enough water to cover them.

Step 3

Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer the wheat berries for about 1 hour, or until they are soft.

Step 4

Drain the wheat berries and return them to the pot.

Step 5

Add the milk, honey, cinnamon, and ginger to the pot, and stir well.

Step 6

Heat the mixture over a fire, stirring constantly, until it comes to a boil.

Step 7

Reduce the heat and let the mixture simmer for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Step 9

Beat the egg yolks in a separate bowl.

Step 10

Take the pot off the fire and slowly pour in the beaten egg yolks, whisking constantly to prevent curdling.

Step 11

Return the pot to the fire and cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.

Step 12

Remove the frumenty pudding from the fire and pour it into a serving bowl.

Step 13

Serve the frumenty pudding hot, garnished with a sprinkle of cinnamon, if desired.

Note:For a creamier frumenty pudding, you can substitute some or all of the milk with cream.
